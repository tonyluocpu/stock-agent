# Dividend Yield Standards & Guidelines

## 📊 What is Dividend Yield?

**Dividend Yield** = (Annual Dividend per Share / Current Stock Price) × 100

It represents the percentage return on investment from dividends alone (excluding capital gains).

---

## 🎯 Market Standards

### Overall Market Average
- **S&P 500**: ~1.5% - 2.5%
- **Dow Jones**: ~2.0% - 3.0%
- **Market Average**: ~2.0%

### What's Considered "Good"?

| Yield Range | Rating | Notes |
|------------|--------|-------|
| **< 1%** | Low | Common for growth stocks (Tech, Biotech) |
| **1% - 2%** | Below Average | Below market average, but acceptable |
| **2% - 4%** | **Good** ✅ | **Above market average, solid income** |
| **4% - 6%** | High | Excellent for income investors, check sustainability |
| **> 6%** | Very High ⚠️ | May indicate risk or unsustainability |

## 🏢 Sector-Specific Standards

### High Dividend Sectors (3%+ typical)

1. **Utilities** 🔌
   - Typical: 3% - 5%
   - Why: Stable, regulated businesses with consistent cash flow
   - Examples: DUK, SO, NEE

2. **REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)** 🏢
   - Typical: 4% - 8%
   - Why: Required to pay 90%+ of taxable income as dividends
   - Examples: O, VNQ, SPG

3. **Consumer Staples** 🛒
   - Typical: 2% - 4%
   - Why: Stable demand, mature businesses
   - Examples: KO, PG, JNJ

4. **Energy** ⛽
   - Typical: 3% - 6%
   - Why: Cash-generative businesses, mature industry
   - Examples: XOM, CVX

5. **Financials** 💰
   - Typical: 2% - 4%
   - Why: Regulated dividends, but cyclical
   - Examples: JPM, BAC, WFC

### Low Dividend Sectors (< 2% typical)

1. **Technology** 💻
   - Typical: < 1% (many pay none)
   - Why: Growth-focused, reinvest profits
   - Examples: AAPL (0.5%), MSFT (0.7%), GOOGL (0%)

2. **Healthcare/Biotech** 🧬
   - Typical: 1% - 2%
   - Why: R&D intensive, growth-focused
   - Examples: JNJ (2.8%), PFE (3.5%), but many pay none

3. **Consumer Discretionary** 🛍️
   - Typical: 1% - 2%
   - Why: Growth and expansion focus
   - Examples: AMZN (0%), TSLA (0%)

## ✅ Good Dividend Criteria

### For Income Investors (2% - 4%+)
- **Above market average** (2%+)
- **Sustainable payout ratio** (< 60% of earnings)
- **Consistent payment history** (5+ years)
- **Growing dividends** (dividend growth rate > inflation)

### For Growth Investors (< 2%)
- **Low/no dividend** is acceptable
- **Reinvesting profits** for growth
- **High ROE** (Return on Equity)

### Red Flags ⚠️

1. **Very High Yield (> 6%)**
   - May indicate:
     - Falling stock price (yield = dividend/price)
     - Unsustainable payout
     - Company in distress

2. **High Payout Ratio (> 80%)**
   - Company paying out most earnings
   - Little room for growth or emergencies

3. **Inconsistent Payments**
   - Cutting dividends = bad sign
   - Irregular payments = cash flow issues

4. **High Debt + High Dividend**
   - Paying dividends while heavily indebted
   - May need to cut dividends to pay debt

## 💡 Investment Strategy Context

### Dividend Investing Strategy
- **Target**: 3% - 5% yield
- **Focus**: Income generation
- **Sectors**: Utilities, REITs, Consumer Staples
- **Risk**: Lower growth potential

### Growth Investing Strategy
- **Target**: < 1% or no dividend
- **Focus**: Capital appreciation
- **Sectors**: Technology, Healthcare, Consumer Discretionary
- **Risk**: Higher volatility

### Balanced Strategy
- **Target**: 2% - 3% yield
- **Focus**: Income + Growth
- **Mix**: Dividend payers + growth stocks
- **Risk**: Moderate

## 🎯 Summary

**Good Dividend Rate Standards:**
- **General Market**: 2% - 4% is considered good
- **Income Focus**: 3% - 5% is excellent
- **Growth Focus**: < 1% is acceptable
- **Sector Context**: Compare to sector average

**Key Takeaway**: 
- A "good" dividend depends on your investment strategy and the sector
- **2% - 4%** is generally considered a solid dividend yield
- Always check **sustainability** (payout ratio, debt, growth) not just the yield number
